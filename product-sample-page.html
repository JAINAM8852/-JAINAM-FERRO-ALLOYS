Manufacturing Process & Heat Treatment of AISI 4140 Round Bar

AISI 4140 alloy steel round bars are manufactured using controlled forging and heat treatment processes to achieve the desired combination of strength, toughness, and machinability. The heat treatment cycle plays a critical role in defining the final mechanical properties of the material.

Annealing of AISI 4140 Round Bar

Annealing of AISI 4140 round stock is carried out at approximately 872°C (1600°F), followed by slow and controlled cooling in the furnace. This process relieves internal stresses, improves ductility, and enhances machinability prior to further processing.

Forging of AISI 4140 Steel Round Bar

Forging of AISI 4140 alloy steel is typically performed within a temperature range of 926°C to 1205°C (1700°F to 2200°F). Proper forging refines the grain structure, improves mechanical strength, and ensures uniform material properties throughout the cross-section.

Hardening & Tempering of AISI 4140 Round Bar

Process Temperature Range (°C) Quenching Medium Purpose
Hardening 830 – 860 °C Oil Increase hardness and tensile strength
Tempering 550 – 660 °C Air Improve toughness and reduce brittleness
Normalizing 830 – 860 °C Air Refine grain structure and enhance uniformity

The combined hardening and tempering process ensures that AISI 4140 round bars achieve excellent wear resistance, fatigue strength, and dimensional stability, making them suitable for heavy-duty engineering and structural applications.
